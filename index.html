Recent Projects

Under-Grad Project

Facial Recognition with Face Masks on Embedded Platform

Amidst the global COVID-19 pandemic, the necessity for effective facial recognition technology that accommodates face masks became paramount. My final year project at the University of Pretoria (TUKS) aimed to address this challenge by developing a facial recognition system capable of accurately identifying individuals wearing face masks in real-time. This system was implemented on an Odroid N2+ platform using C++ programming language, chosen for its efficiency and control over system resources.

Masters Project

Multimodal Emotion Recognition Using AI Techniques

For my master's thesis, I delved into the exploration of artificial intelligence techniques for emotion recognition, employing multiple data modalities. The project's ambition was to create a system that could accurately identify and classify human emotions by analyzing varied sources of input data, including audio, visual, and physiological signals. The cornerstone of this system was a late fusion model, which intelligently combined the outputs of distinct AI models, each processing a different modality of data, to arrive at a comprehensive understanding of the subject's emotional state. This system was designed and implemented using the python language combined with TensorFlow and OpenCV.

Personal Project

Triangulating the Distance of Farm Animals Using ESPs

For our project titled "Triangulating the Distance of Farm Animals Using ESPs," we leveraged the advanced WiFi capabilities of ESP embedded systems to meticulously triangulate distances between a central pole and various farm animals in its vicinity. This innovative endeavor aimed to enhance the analysis and tracking of cattle within agricultural environments using the Signal strengh. By employing this technology, we developed a highly efficient method to monitor livestock movement and distribution across farms, offering valuable insights for farm management and operational optimization.
